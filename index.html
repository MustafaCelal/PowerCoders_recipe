Menemen

Traditional Turkish Scrambled Eggs

Menemen is a beloved Turkish breakfast dish featuring eggs gently scrambled with tomatoes, peppers, and aromatic spices. This comforting one-pan meal is perfect for lazy weekend mornings and captures the essence of Turkish home cooking.
Prep Time 10 minutes
Cook Time 15 minutes
Servings 2-3 people
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 2 green peppers (or 1 bell pepper), chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil or butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sautÃ© until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the chopped peppers to the pan and cook for another 3-4 minutes until they begin to soften.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they break down and release their juices. Season with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.
  4. Crack the eggs directly into the pan over the tomato mixture. Gently stir to scramble the eggs, mixing them with the vegetables. Cook until the eggs are just set but still creamy, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat immediately to prevent overcooking. Garnish with fresh parsley and crumbled feta cheese if desired.
  6. Serve hot with crusty bread, olives, and Turkish tea for an authentic breakfast experience.
